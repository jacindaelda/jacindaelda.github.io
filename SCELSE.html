Summer Internship at SCELSE

Research Topic: Anaerobic Digestion Ecosystem Microbial Observatory (ADEMO) of Ulu Pandan Water Reclamation Plant

About SCELSE

SCELSE (Singapore Centre for Environmental Life Sciences Engineering) is an interdisciplinary Research Centre of Excellence. SCELSE research focus on studying about microbial biofilm and utilizing them for medical and environmental preservation purpose.

From Left to Right: Me, Anika (the Ph.D. student I worked with), Anisa (Anika's sister), Olivia (My co-worker)

Organic chemicals in sludge have been a major concern for public health. Some are toxic to organisms, while others have a foul stench. Some also disrupt the ecological balance, thus endangering the population inside. Therefore, sludge should be treated before disposal.

The treatment process involves the employment of bacteria to digest the nutrients in the sludge. SRT (Solid Retention Time) is an important parameter that relates the growth of microorganisms and the effluent concentrations. An effective SRT enhances nutrients removal with a low operating cost.

This project investigates the impact of changing the SRT to the microbial community within the digester and the subsequent chemical balances of the effluent. It is hoped that understanding the relations between these three variables can lead to the improvement of the currently available sludge treatment system.

Activities

I mainly took samples and measured the sludge concentrations. These give me a hands-on wet lab experience. In details, my job includes:

  • Performing COD Test on the Discharged Sludge Using TNT Test Kit

    • COD (Chemical Oxygen Demand) is an index that indicates the level of oxidizable organic materials in the sludge. Higher COD level means that lesser oxygen dissolves in the discharged wastewater. This lead to an anaerobic condition which endangers the higher aquatic life forms.

  • Performing Ammonia Test on the Discharged Sludge Using TNT Test Kit

    • Ammonia is generated from the decomposition of nitrogenous compounds (i.e. protein). High level of ammonia inhibits the activity of methanogens, which is one of the major players in the digestor. As a result, the reactor becomes less effective in removing nutrients.

  • Performing Alkalinity Test on the Discharged Sludge Using TNT Test Kit

    • Volatile acid is released into the system as the sludge is being degraded. Alkalinity test indicates the system's ability to resist a massive drop in pH. In a "too sour" condition, digesters cannot work properly hence no digestion takes place in the reactor.

  • Performing VFA Test on the Discharged Sludge Using TNT Test Kit and Preparing Gas Samples for Gas Chromatography

    • The analysis of Volatile Fatty Acid (VFA) concentrations allows an early detection of digestion upset. For example, the accumulation of acetate is the indicator of stress for methanogens.

  • Extracting SMP and EPS from the Sludge with Vacuum Filter and Heater

    • Digesters excrete SMP (Soluble Microbial Products) and EPS (Extracellular Polymeric Substances) as the by-products of the digestion. SMP and EPS have been shown to influence various properties of activated sludge such as floc size distribution, viscosity, dewaterability, and compressibility. The purpose of this filtration is to separate SMP from EPS and analyze both of them separately since SMP has different chemical properties from EPS.
